Our Mission

Colin's Kids' mission is to provide funding to advance medical research related to congenital heart defects and to provide financial assistance to families struggling to obtain the best care for their children.

Research Grants

Funded 21 grants totaling $180,000+ for congenital heart defect research at Columbia and Duke

Infant Scales

Provided scales to 130+ families reducing costly trips to the hospital for routine weight checks.

Blanket Donations

Comforted 1,000+ children recovering from open heart surgery with hand-made fleece blankets

Patient Aid

Provided significant financial assistance to CHD families struggling to make ends meet

Awareness Initiatives

Raised awareness for CHD by hosting both large-scale fundraisers and local school events

Fundraising Events

Raising funds to support initiatives through golf outings, dinners and auctions.

Student Chapters

Partnering with schools and community organizations to fundraise and organize blanket making events

Our History

In the Spring of 2008, two families met in the Neonatal Intensive Care Unit at Morgan Stanley Children's Hospital of New York-Presbyterian. Their children, Colin and Andrew, were born with congenital heart defects ("CHDs"), and would spend the first weeks of their lives recovering from heart surgeries performed shortly after birth. Andrew survived a transposition of the great arteries, thanks to an arterial switch procedure that did not exist until thirty years ago. Colin, born with hypoplastic left heart syndrome, died from surgical complications after only 47 days, despite the best efforts of the dedicated staff at Morgan Stanley Children's Hospital. The families founded Colin's Kids in memory of Colin, whose brief time nevertheless brought incredible joy to his family and friends, and in honor of Andrew, Colin's first and best buddy.
