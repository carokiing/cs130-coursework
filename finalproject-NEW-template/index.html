Our History

In the Spring of 2008, two families met in the Neonatal Intensive Care Unit at Morgan Stanley Children's Hospital of New York-Presbyterian. Their children, Colin and Andrew, were born with congenital heart defects ("CHDs"), and would spend the first weeks of their lives recovering from heart surgeries performed shortly after birth. Andrew survived a transposition of the great arteries, thanks to an arterial switch procedure that did not exist until thirty years ago. Colin, born with hypoplastic left heart syndrome, died from surgical complications after only 47 days, despite the best efforts of the dedicated staff at Morgan Stanley Children's Hospital. The families founded Colin's Kids in memory of Colin, whose brief time nevertheless brought incredible joy to his family and friends, and in honor of Andrew, Colin's first and best buddy.
